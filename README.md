# WorthCrunch — Solar Savings & Payback Calculator (2026)

**Live:** https://worthcrunchhq.github.io/

A free, 100% client-side calculator that estimates home-solar **cost, payback period, and 25-year
savings** — updated for **2026**, after the U.S. federal residential solar tax credit (Section 25D)
**expired for systems placed in service after Dec 31, 2025**. Most calculators online still bake in
the old 30% credit and now show the wrong numbers; this one models the post-credit reality
(cash / loan / lease / PPA), by state, with **every assumption visible and editable** — and **no
email, no signup, no tracking of your inputs**.

## 📊 Open dataset (free, CC BY 4.0)

`data/solar-by-state-2026.json` — estimated **2026 solar cost, payback, and 25-year savings for all
50 states + DC** (average home, cash purchase), plus the underlying peak-sun-hours and average
electricity rates. Free to reuse **with attribution / a link back** to WorthCrunch.

```
GET https://worthcrunchhq.github.io/data/solar-by-state-2026.json
```

Useful for journalists, bloggers, and researchers covering the 2026 solar tax-credit change.

## How it works

`annual production ≈ system_kW × peak_sun_hours × 365 × 0.83 derate × shading`, sized to offset ~100%
of usage; net cost after incentives (federal = $0 for 2026 cash/loan); savings grown 3%/yr for
electricity inflation, reduced 0.5%/yr for panel degradation; payback = year cumulative savings exceed
net cost. Full methodology on the site. Estimates use state-average data — a transparent ballpark,
not an engineering quote.

## Tech

Static HTML/CSS/JS, no build step, no backend, hosted free on GitHub Pages. Data and pages are
generated by `build/gen-states.js`.
